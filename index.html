The Big Mango

The Big Mango is a 10-meter-tall, bright orange fiberglass sculpture located in Bowen, Queensland, Australia. It was built in 2002 to celebrate Bowens reputation as a major mango-producing region, particularly known for the Kensington Pride mango. As one of Australias many "Big Things," it serves as a popular roadside attraction and photo spot for visitors traveling through North Queensland.

History of The Big Mango

History

The Big Mango in Bowen, Queensland, is a giant roadside attraction celebrating the towns reputation as the mango capital of Australia. Heres a bit of its history:
Unveiled in 2002, the 10-meter (33 ft) tall, 7-tonne fibreglass structure was built to promote Bowens famous Kensington Pride mangoes, which are a major part of the regions agricultural industry.
It is located at the Bowen Visitor Information Centre, just off the Bruce Highway, about 4 km south of Bowen.
The Big Mango was briefly "stolen" in 2014 as part of a clever publicity stunt by fast-food chain Nandos.
The disappearance of the massive structure made headlines across Australia, but it was later revealed to be a marketing campaign.
Today, it serves as a popular photo stop for tourists and travelers, with a nearby shop selling mango sorbet, jams, and other local treats.
